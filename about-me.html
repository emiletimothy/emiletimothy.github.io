Hello! My name is Emile Timothy. I'm a senior Computer Science major at Caltech from Dubai and I am passionate about creating new technologies. On campus, I run for the collegiate cross country and track teams, run the Caltech Arnis Club as the Vice President, and am a member of the Caltech Data Science team. I am also involved with Title IX advocacy, the International Student office programs and the Physics-11 class through which I had a summer research fellowship with Caltech LIGO and Professor Rana Adhikari through the Tombrello and Brewer fellowships. My other interests include working on research or coding projects, playing the Piano, thinking about wild hypothetical scenarios, and exploring boba stores and trails around Pasadena and Los Angeles. I occasionally post pictures and share my thoughts on Instagram (@emiletimothy). A general list of my accomplishments and the projects I've worked on can be found here.

My research with Professor Umans focuses on constructing a framework to study the model of random walks on expander graphs. A prior result shows that the sticky random walk can be a useful model to understand the full-scale expander graph. My research is devoted to generalizing the sticky random walk from 2 symbols to m symbols, using a generalized Krawtchouk function and combinatorial arguments. My research with Professor Wierman focuses on using perturbation analysis to prove stability bounds on using model-predictive control on linear time-varying (LTV) systems in which the predictions of the disturbances are noisy, and extending this to linear feedback policies on the regularized online balanced descent (R-OBD) algorithm. Additionally, I am creating simulations to show that perturbing the target parameters of noisy LTV systems leads to large deviations from the optimal cost.

Prior to coming to Caltech, I represented the United Arab Emirates in the International Physics Olympiad (IPhO), did research on classical electromagnetic radiation in various topologies, built a solar-powered RC race-car, and served my community through volunteering and leadership activities.
