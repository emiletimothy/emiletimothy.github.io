Proving the Stability of Controlling Noisy Dynamical Systems

This summer, I'm working with Professor Adam Wierman and graduate student Yiheng Lin at the Rigorous Systems Research Group (RSRG) at Caltech, on a research project that is funded by the Caltech Associates SURF Fellowship. My research with Prof. Wierman focuses on using perturbation analysis to prove stability bounds on using model-predictive control on linear time-varying (LTV) systems in which the predictions of the disturbances are noisy, and extending this to linear feedback policies on the regularized online balanced descent (R-OBD) algorithm.
